Scotch Eggs with Mustard Aioli

Ingredients:

  • 5-6 Eggs
  • 1 lb Pork Based Sausage
  • 1 Cup Breadcrumbs (I Prefer Panko)
  • Optional:Frying Compatible Oil (Vegetable, Canola, Peanut)
  • 3/4 Cups All Purpose Flour
  • 3 TBS Stone Ground Mustard
  • 1/2 Cup Mayonnaise
  • 1 TSP Honey
  • 3 Cloves of Garlic, Crushed into Paste
  • Salt
  • Freshly Ground Black Pepper
  • 1 TSP Paprika
  • Optional:1/4 - 1 Cup Black Pudding

Directions

Scotch Eggs

  1. Hard boil all but one of the eggs. Cool and peel them.
  2. If baking the Scotch Eggs then preheat an oven to 400ËšF.
  3. Prepare Mustard Sauce now if not already prepared, continue while sauce is chilled.
  4. If the sausage is in a casing cut open casing and squeeze out the meat into a mixing bowl.
  5. If using the black pudding then mix in the desired amount into the sausage meat.
  6. Mix in a large pinch of salt and pepper along with a clove of garlic into the meat.
  7. Coat each boiled egg in the sausage mixture, evenly coating each of them.
  8. Arrange three containers.
  9. Put the flour along with the paprika in one tray, combine.
  10. Beat the remaining egg and place in tray.
  11. Put Breadcrumbs into remaining tray
  12. Cover a sausage coated egg in seasoned flour, then egg, then breadcrumbs. Repeat with others.
  13. If baking, place Scotch Eggs onto baking sheet and into oven for approximately 35 minutes or until sausage is no longer pink near egg.
  14. If frying, put enough oil into a frying pan to cover bottom, about 2 TBS (Enough for frying).
  15. Fry the Scotch Eggs until there is no pink meat near the egg, a few minutes per side.
  16. Take out of pan/sheet and let cool. Scotch Eggs are good hot or cold, most often eaten cold.

    17. Mustard Aioli

    1. Whisk the mustard, mayonnaise, honey and garlic. Add a pinch of salt and pepper.
    2. Cover mixing bowl and refrigerate for a minimum of 30 minutes to an entire day.
